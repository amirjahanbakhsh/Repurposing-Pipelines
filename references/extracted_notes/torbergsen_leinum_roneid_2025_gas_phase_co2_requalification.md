## Why This Paper Matters

This is a core gas-phase CO2 repurposing source.

It is important because many existing oil and gas pipelines may not be suitable for dense-phase CO2 due to original design pressure or fracture arrest capacity. Gas-phase CO2 may therefore be the more realistic reuse route for some assets.

## Model-Relevant Points

- Dense phase is often preferred for new large CO2 pipelines, but it may not be feasible for reuse.
- The paper reports that a high-level European screening found many existing oil and gas pipelines technically suitable for gas-phase CO2, but only a small fraction suitable for dense phase.
- Individual pipelines still need pipeline-specific requalification.
- Reuse cost saving depends strongly on the modification work needed to conclude compliance.
- DNV-SE-0657 is cited as the route for pipeline-system requalification for hydrogen and CO2.
- CO2SafePipe JIP is cited as addressing gas and dense CO2 repurposing challenges.
- Gas-phase CO2 experience is more limited than dense phase, but examples exist.
- OCAP uses an approximately 100 km, 26 inch requalified vintage oil pipeline operating in gas phase at about 7-21 bara and around 0.5 Mtpa.
- The LACQ CCS pilot used an approximately 30 km, 12/8 inch onshore pipeline originally designed for natural gas and converted to CO2.
- Water specification is critical. The note reports OCAP water specification below 40 ppm-mol.
- Lower temperature can lower water solubility and require stricter water limits.

## How We Should Use It

Use this paper to strengthen our phase decision logic:

| Decision | Model implication |
| --- | --- |
| Dense phase feasible | Check pressure, toughness, decompression and running ductile fracture. |
| Dense phase not feasible | Assess gas-phase CO2 capacity and arrival pressure. |
| Gas phase selected | Apply stricter water specification and lower capacity expectations. |
| Water limit unknown | Do not pass final gate; mark as sensitivity only. |
| Modification scope high | Compare reuse against new-build rather than assuming reuse wins. |
