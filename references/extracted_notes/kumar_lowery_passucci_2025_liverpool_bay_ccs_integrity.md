## Why This Paper Matters

This paper is a practical UK CCS pipeline case for Liverpool Bay.

It is especially useful because it makes cleaning, drying, residual debris and inspection very concrete. This helps us convert the word `refurbishment` into actual work items.

## Model-Relevant Points

- The Liverpool Bay CCS project includes new pipelines and repurposed existing pipelines.
- Existing Liverpool Bay wet gas pipelines are planned for CO2 service, initially gas phase and later dense phase.
- ILI campaigns in 2020/2023 identified residual solid debris in six gas pipelines planned for repurposing.
- Debris can permit local water hold-up, increasing corrosion risk.
- Mechanical and chemical cleaning plus drying are required before CO2 service.
- The paper highlights achieving a very low dew point before CO2 operation.
- MFL inspection is used to identify corrosion, defects and damage.
- e-Splora, video inspection and other tools are discussed for evaluating cleanliness and integrity.
- The paper supports a full integrity management cycle: risk assessment, inspection, maintenance, testing, monitoring, audit, emergency response and repairs.

## How We Should Use It

Use this paper to create real work items in the repurposing gate:

| Work item | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| `mechanical_cleaning_required` | Removes debris that can hold water. |
| `chemical_cleaning_required` | May be needed if mechanical cleaning is insufficient. |
| `drying_required` | CO2 service needs a very dry line. |
| `dew_point_target_known` | The model should not hide dryness assumptions. |
| `ili_mfl_available` | Supports condition assessment before reuse. |
| `video_or_cleanliness_inspection_required` | Confirms cleanliness before CO2 service. |
| `residual_debris_risk` | Direct corrosion trigger for reuse. |

## Limitations

- The paper is a project case and does not provide a universal formula.
- It supports screening and work-scope design rather than final approval.
