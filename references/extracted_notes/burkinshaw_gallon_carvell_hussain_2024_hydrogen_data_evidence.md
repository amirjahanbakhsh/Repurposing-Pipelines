## Why This Paper Matters

This paper is hydrogen-focused, so it is not a direct CO2 source.

However, it is very useful for our data traceability philosophy. It shows how National Gas Transmission and ROSEN approach evidence gathering, data quality and data gap closure before repurposing pipelines.

## Model-Relevant Points

- Repurposing assessment depends on complete and accurate data.
- Primary records should be preferred over secondary records.
- Secondary records can contain copied assumptions, mistakes or missing information about later repairs and reroutes.
- Data should be ranked by quality, confidence and reliability.
- GIS databases can be used to manage and visualise pipeline evidence.
- Data gaps can be closed using ILI, in-situ testing and destructive testing.
- The paper lists useful datasets:
  - design temperature and pressure;
  - design factors;
  - operating pressure and gas composition;
  - material properties;
  - coating types;
  - hydrotest pressures;
  - girth weld types;
  - weld procedure qualification records;
  - construction NDE;
  - faults, incidents and repairs;
  - CP survey data;
  - route profile and crossings;
  - ILI, direct assessment and NDE data.

## How We Should Use It

Use this paper to improve our evidence-confidence system.

For our model, each key input should have:

- value;
- unit;
- source;
- source type;
- confidence;
- whether it is primary or secondary evidence;
- whether it needs verification before the pipeline can pass the final gate.

## Limitations

- This paper is about hydrogen repurposing, not CO2.
- Hydrogen material degradation should not be copied into CO2 calculations.
- The data-quality lessons are transferable.
