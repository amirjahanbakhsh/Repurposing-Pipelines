## Why This Paper Matters

This paper is useful for the post-screening part of our model: inspection, maintenance and repair, or IMR.

It also supports the idea that new CO2 transport conditions create both changed old threats and new threats compared with conventional oil and gas pipelines.

## Model-Relevant Points

- CO2 may be transported in gas, liquid or supercritical/dense phase.
- Impurities such as H2S, nitrogen and water can affect phase behaviour and corrosion.
- Free water with CO2 and impurities is a key carbon-steel corrosion risk.
- Safety systems such as overpressure protection and leak detection may need specialised design.
- The paper supports building a baseline benchmark before detailed risk assessment.
- A risk-based IMR plan should include threat identification, risk assessment, inspection, monitoring, testing, integrity assessment and repairs.
- A real case is discussed for a 34 inch offshore pipeline with existing corrosion defects and 3LPE external coating.
- Fitness-for-purpose assessment affects whether existing defects need intervention under CO2 service.

## Limitations

- It gives IMR planning logic, not a simple screening equation.
- The real case is illustrative and should not be directly copied to UK pipelines without matching conditions.
