## Why This Paper Matters

This is a core source for our CO2 repurposing foundation.

It clearly states that changing the transported product to CO2 requires requalification. It also links the logic to DNV-ST-F101, DNV-RP-F104 and ISO 27913.

## Model-Relevant Points

- Reuse is attractive, but technical, safety and financial risks must be addressed.
- CO2 pipeline requalification needs to confirm safety, operability, integrity and transport capacity.
- CO2 composition matters. The paper notes that industry specifications may range around 95-99% CO2, with impurities affecting corrosion and fracture control.
- Free water must be avoided because CO2 plus water can cause high internal corrosion rates in carbon-manganese steel.
- Sulfur, oxygen and other impurities can further change corrosion risk.
- Dense-phase CO2 can create demanding running ductile fracture requirements.
- The original Battelle Two-Curve Method is described as non-conservative for dense-phase CO2.
- Some dense-phase CO2 cases may require very high Charpy V-notch toughness, potentially above 300 J.
- Older pipelines may struggle to document this toughness, especially where material certificates are missing.

## Requalification Steps Extracted

The paper describes a requalification process with these steps:

| Step | Meaning for our model |
| --- | --- |
| 1. Initiation | Establish design basis, modifications, operating history, battery limits, material selection, pressure rating, CO2 capacity, delivery pressure, CO2 state and composition. |
| 2. Integrity assessment | Establish current technical condition and historical operation before changing service. |
| 3. Hydraulic analysis and flow assurance | Check CO2 transport capacity, pressure, temperature and operating cases. |
| 4. Safety evaluations | Check accidental release, block valves, leak detection, component suitability and consequence radius. |
| 5. Define CO2 transport premises | Define design/incidental pressure, receiving pressure and functional requirements. |
| 6. Reassessment | Recheck integrity under the new CO2 premises and identify non-compliant parts. |
| 7. Modifications | Identify mitigations, repairs, material testing, impurity limits and operating envelope changes. |
| 8. Document | Update documentation, drawings, equipment lists and operating procedures. |
| 9. Implement | Complete modifications before CO2 operation begins. |

## How We Should Use It

Use this paper to strengthen our pre-LCA gate and validation gate.

It supports:

- requiring CO2 state and composition as model inputs;
- treating missing original design basis and material certificates as major evidence gaps;
- separating gas-phase and dense-phase screening;
- adding fracture and impurity checks before cost and LCA;
- passing only itemised modifications into cost and LCA.

## Limitations

- It is a conference paper, not the full DNV-RP-F104 standard.
- It supports screening and requalification logic, but final approval still requires project-specific engineering evidence.
